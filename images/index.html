Cthulhu Rising

The legend of Cthulhu originates from the works of H.P. Lovecraft, first introduced in his 1928 short story The Call of Cthulhu. Described as a monstrous cosmic entity with an octopus-like head, immense wings, and a grotesque, humanoid form, Cthulhu lies dormant beneath the Pacific Ocean in the sunken city of R'lyeh, awaiting the time when "the stars are right" for its awakening. Worshiped by cultists and feared as an ancient, god-like being, Cthulhu represents existential horror, embodying the insignificance of humanity in the face of incomprehensible cosmic forces. The phrase "Ph’nglui mglw’nafh Cthulhu R’lyeh wgah’nagl fhtagn"—roughly translating to "In his house at R’lyeh, dead Cthulhu waits dreaming"—has become iconic in Lovecraftian horror, symbolizing the lurking dread of the unknown and the inevitable return of eldritch horrors beyond human understanding.
